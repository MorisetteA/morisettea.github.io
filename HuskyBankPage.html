HuskyBank

Purpose

HuskyBank is your financial companion to prepare you for the “real world”. Starting college is already a hard transition to go through, being in debt makes it harder. HuskyBank presents students the opportunity to play with the idea of budgeting. As they play, students learn the tools they need to manage finances responsibly in a less-intimidating and interactive experience.

Problem

As university students try to manage their school work, jobs, and social lives, there is a tendency to forget how to properly manage their money. The financial stress accumulates and puts a greater mental strain on the students, negatively affecting the overall well-being of the students.

Research

Competitve Analysis

Since we weren't familiar with how to design a popular and intuitive budgeting app, our first step was to look at the reviews and comments of the top 6 budgeting/finance related apps. There was also a focus on what the general users believe the pros and cons of each respective app.

App Inspirations

  • Intuit Mint
  • Pocket Guard
  • Mvelopes
  • Wally
  • Simple
  • GoodBudget

Pros

  • Alerts regarding bill reminder, late fees, over budget in a catergory
  • Saves past locations for quicker future entry
  • assiging recurring income and expenses
  • taking photos of receipts to store them
  • basic overview of the users spending habits

Cons

  • Budget goes restarts to zero every month
  • Free and premium versions
  • Certain app features are too complicated to input information
  • Unable to make money input edits
  • Constant fixing or reorganizing budget categories can be overwhelming

Interviews

To gain a greater insight into what university students are experiencing regarding financial difficulties and budgeting app background, we decided to conduct interviews with university students. The responses we were most interested in was what the students look for in a budgeting app. The responses mainly focused on:

  • Simplicity and easy usability
  • Incentive to use
  • Aesthetically pleasing / fun
  • Categorization of different expenses / budgeting mechanic
  • Progress reports / spending trends
  • Privacy / security of information
  • Financial resources

Personas

With the new information we received, we began to form the outline for who our audience was. We ended up splitting the audience into 3 main personas. The 3 personas ranged from our optimal target audience to our least targeted audience. I created our optimal target persona "Samantha" intending to describe her as the typical student who was in a financial struggle with the hopes of getting more organized.

Low Fidelity Wireframes

Husky Avatar

We wanted our budgeting app to stand out from the other available budgeting apps. To do this, we came up with the idea of having an avatar for the user to interact with when using the app. We wanted an avatar who could be viewed as a friend that encourages the user. With that in mind, a cute Husky dog that acted as the student's financial companion was chosen to be the avatar. The Husky dog breed was chosen to be the avatar specifically because that breed is the mascot of the University of Washington, and our app is catered towards the University of Washington students. To make the app more appealing, we decided to design the Husky in a cartoonish style.

High Fidelity Wireframe

Current Prototype Iteration

The main changes made from our high-fidelity wireframe was having the background color be more subtle and have the vibrant colors be accents. We decided to separate the information with rounded squares to avoid having a block of information that could overwhelm the user. Adding the gray paw prints in the background was another decision we made to fill in the white space and, in turn, making the app look playful.

Lessons + Moving Forward

This project was my first project where the teammates all had various skills. There was an even split between the coders and the artists, and with this came some challenges in understanding what was most important in the development process and what needed the bulk of our attention. Overcoming these challenges took a bit of time as we had to learn one another's limits.

When the idea was first created, our final goal was to have a functional app to be used by the students of the University of Washington. That is still our goal. Our next step is to do a final redesign of the application and pitch the idea to the University.
